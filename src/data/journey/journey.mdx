# My Journey

A look back at the projects, opportunities, and experiences that have shaped my growth as a developer, engineer, and student.

## 2026: Building for Real Users

* **June:** Joined **FischerJordan** as a Software Development Engineering Intern, contributing to backend systems and production engineering workflows.
* **May:** Launched **UniPool**, a ride-sharing platform built for the NIT Delhi community. The project was officially launched in the presence of the Director and Registrar and is actively used by students, faculty, and staff.
* **March:** Continued contributing to the **Photography Club**, documenting campus events and preserving moments across institute life.
* **Ongoing:** Exploring backend systems, distributed architectures, and machine learning infrastructure through projects and internships.

## 2025: Discovering AI Engineering

* **August:** Selected for **Amazon ML Summer School 2025**, studying machine learning, deep learning, NLP, and generative AI alongside students from across the country.
* **July – September:** Joined **Soundverse AI** as an Applied AI Intern, working on agentic AI systems, MCP integrations, and AI-powered music generation workflows.
* **May:** Built **VocaAI**, an AI receptionist capable of handling customer calls and common business inquiries through real-time voice interactions.
* **Sparkathon 2025:** Developed a **Real-Time Bot Detection System** designed to identify and block malicious purchases during high-demand sales events.

## 2024: Expanding Beyond Web Development

* Entered my second year at **NIT Delhi** and began exploring machine learning alongside web development.
* Started experimenting with computer vision, AI applications, and backend systems.
* Built projects that combined software engineering fundamentals with emerging AI technologies.
* Became increasingly involved in campus activities through the Photography Club.

## 2023: The Foundation

* Began my **B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering** at the National Institute of Technology Delhi.
* Learned the fundamentals of programming, web development, and modern JavaScript ecosystems.
* Built my first projects with React and Next.js.
* Joined the Photography Club and started documenting campus life through photography.
* Competed in powerlifting and achieved a personal best total of **365 kg**.

---

Looking back, the biggest shift wasn't moving from web development to AI—it was moving from learning technologies to building products. Each project, internship, and experience has reinforced the same lesson: software becomes meaningful when it solves a real problem for real people.
